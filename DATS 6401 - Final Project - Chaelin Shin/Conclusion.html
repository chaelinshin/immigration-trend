Key Findings

  • The number of refugees increased greatly in Turkey and moderately in the U.S.
  • There was increased number of apprehensions in the U.S., which may be due to the current administration's return-to-Mexico policy.
  • The numbers of greencard-holders, H1B visas, or F1 visas have not changed much. This may be because the regulations regarding these categories cannot be easily changed due to vested interests of schools and companies.

    • Conclusion

    As expected, the number of refugees or number of apprehension have changed significantly after President Trump assumed office. However, there were some unexpected results too. For example, the numbers of removals or returns have not increased as much as the number of apprehensions, and the numbers were even sometimes lower than the numbers during the Obama administration.
